Appendix A  ·  Methodology

Applicant Journey Flow Methodology

Technical documentation of the data pipeline, path construction, and visualization approach used in the Applicant Journey Flow visualization.

Section 1

Overview

The Applicant Journey Flow visualization maps every distinct sequence of decisions an applicant can move through — from initial application, through lottery, acceptance, registration, and enrollment, to retention outcomes. Because applicants can reach the same outcome via different paths, this visualization treats the journey as a directed graph rather than a simple funnel.

Why paths matter A simple funnel collapses the heterogeneity in how applicants arrive at outcomes. Two applicants who both enrolled may have followed very different sequences. This visualization preserves that nuance by encoding full path sequences.

Section 4

Visualization Design

The visualization consists of three linked panels:

A
Top Ranked Applicant Outcomes
Shows the 8 most common journey paths for the current filter. Bars are clickable to select a path and highlight it across all panels. When selected, raw count bars dim and a proportion overlay appears.
B
Final Outcomes
Aggregates all applicants by their terminal outcome. When a path is selected, an overlay shows how applicants in that path distribute across outcomes, with a proportion label.
C
Applicant Journey Flow Diagram
A directed node-link diagram connecting every decision stage. Edge thickness encodes the number of applicants on each transition. Selecting a path illuminates its route through the diagram.
Section 5

Filtering

Three independent filters update all panels simultaneously:

  • Year — filter to a specific academic year (2023, 2024, 2025) or view all years combined
  • School — filter to any specific NHA school by name
  • Grade — filter to a specific grade level (Pre-K through 12)
Data Access Statement — Data supporting this analysis cannot be made available due to client confidentiality and NDA restrictions. Code and methodological details are provided to enable replication with comparable data.
